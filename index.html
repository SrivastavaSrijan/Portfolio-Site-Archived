Highly Sensitive D-Type Optical Fiber

Capstone Project

As a team of two, we designed a surface plasmon resonance fiber sensor and it was simulated, modeled and studied on COMSOL.

The sensor used evanescent wave on the surface of the fiber to excite free electrons on the metal surface to generate surface plasmon waves (SPW). The change of refractive index (RI) was measured by monitoring the variation of the resonance absorption peak.

Said sensor used nano-structures to generated Localized Surface Plasmon Resonance(L-SPR). The coating material for the nano-structures was varied from Gold, Silver and ITO (Indium Titanium Oxide) and the resonance peak was monitored.

These simulation results were key to the selection of the material as well as the design and fabrication of the SPR.

Image Steganography using AES

Through encryption & encoding, we proposed a way to hide an encrypted text into an image without altering the image in any shape or form, which is formally known as steganography.

As a team, we worked on many verticals - GUI Design using tkinter, Python, Security and Bug Testing.

We could get the original text back by decryption and decoding. The encryption module encrypts the information using AES (Advanced Encryption Standard) using a key known only to the encoder.

The encoding module takes the cipher text formed by encryption module and an image from the user as input. Using Steganography, more specifically the LSB method, the cipher text is hidden into the image, completely undetectable to anyone.

Syllable Recognition using MATLAB

Using a pre-recorded waveform (template) we were able to accurately detect it in different contexts(samples).

We sampled the input signal to convert it into a digital waveform, and performed noise reduction using a band pass Butterworth filter. Then we aligned the signal by finding the peaks of both the template and the sample signal to adjust for delays.

After this, we performed Fast Fourier Transform. Then, auto correlation and cross correlation was done. Auto correlation was kept as a reference & cross correlation was performed between the sample signal & the template.

When these two signals are similar, we could conclude the template and the sample are the same syllables.
